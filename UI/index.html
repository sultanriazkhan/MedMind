Understanding HbA1c: What Your Diabetes Marker Really Means

DR
Dr. Raza Ahmed
Endocrinologist · MBBS, FCPS

What is HbA1c?

HbA1c, or glycated hemoglobin, is a blood test that measures the percentage of hemoglobin coated with sugar (glycated). Since red blood cells live approximately 3 months, this test reflects your average blood glucose control over that period.

Unlike a fasting glucose test that only captures a single moment in time, HbA1c provides a comprehensive 90-day window into your metabolic health.

Normal vs. Abnormal Ranges

Normal Below 5.7%
Prediabetes 5.7% – 6.4%
Diabetes 6.5% or above

How to Lower It Naturally

  • • Reduce refined carbohydrates and added sugars
  • • Increase dietary fiber (vegetables, legumes, whole grains)
  • • Exercise at least 150 minutes per week (moderate intensity)
  • • Maintain a healthy sleep schedule (7–8 hours)
  • • Monitor blood glucose regularly with a home glucometer
